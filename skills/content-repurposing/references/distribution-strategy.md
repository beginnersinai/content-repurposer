# Distribution Strategy

The strategic framework for content distribution. This is the knowledge base the distribution-planner agent and auto-activating skill draw from.

---

## The Core Principle

Repurposing is not copy-paste. It is re-thinking your content for each platform's native format, audience expectation, and consumption context. The same idea should feel like it was originally written for each platform it appears on.

**The anti-pattern:** Posting identical text to Twitter, LinkedIn, email, and your blog. This performs worse than not posting at all — algorithms detect cross-posting, audiences notice laziness, and engagement craters across every platform.

---

## The Cascade Model

Publish content in a deliberate sequence that builds momentum across platforms. Each stage reaches a different audience in a different context.

### Stage 1: Owned Channels (Day 1)
**Email newsletter first.** Your subscribers opted in. They get the content before anyone else. This:
- Rewards loyalty (they chose to hear from you)
- Generates early engagement signals (opens, clicks, replies)
- Lets you test which hooks and angles resonate before going public

### Stage 2: Long-Form Social (Day 1-2)
**LinkedIn post + X/Twitter thread.** These reach your followers and, through engagement, their networks. Long-form formats let you develop the idea properly.
- Post the LinkedIn version and X thread on the same day or consecutive days
- Each should have a different hook — do not use the same opening line
- LinkedIn: professional angle. X: punchy, insight-forward angle.

### Stage 3: Visual & Engagement Formats (Day 2-3)
**LinkedIn carousel + Community post.** These formats prioritize interaction.
- Carousels generate 2-3x the engagement of text posts and keep people on-platform longer
- Community posts start discussions that build relationships
- These can reference "something I shared yesterday" for cross-platform momentum

### Stage 4: Video Formats (Day 3-5)
**YouTube script + Short-form video.** Video takes longer to produce, so schedule it later.
- YouTube for depth (8-12 minutes)
- TikTok/Reels/Shorts for discovery (30-60 seconds)
- These can reach entirely new audiences who never saw the written versions

### Stage 5: Extended Distribution (Day 5-7)
**Blog summary + Quote graphics + Podcast talking points.** These are the long tail.
- Blog summary for SEO and syndication
- Quote graphics for visual platforms (Instagram, Pinterest)
- Podcast points for audio content or interview prep
- These formats have the longest shelf life and continue generating value weeks later

## The 48-Hour Rule

The same core idea can appear on multiple platforms within 48 hours without feeling stale. After 48 hours, it needs a fresh angle. This is because:

- Platform audiences overlap less than you think (your LinkedIn followers and X followers are mostly different people)
- Different formats present the idea differently enough to feel new
- But after 48 hours, even your multi-platform followers will feel like they've seen this before

**After 48 hours, apply freshness techniques:**
- Change the hook (same idea, different opening)
- Change the angle (focus on a different aspect of the same content)
- Change the format dramatically (text → video, thread → carousel)
- Add new information or an update
- Frame it as a response to engagement ("Last week I shared X. Here's what happened...")

---

## Freshness Techniques

How to make the same content feel new on each platform:

### Change the Hook
Source article opens with a stat → X thread opens with a contrarian take → LinkedIn opens with a story → Email opens with a question. Same destination, four different doors.

### Change the Angle
A blog post about "10 ways to use AI in your business" becomes:
- X thread: "The one way most businesses are NOT using AI"
- LinkedIn: "What I learned auditing 50 businesses for AI readiness"
- Newsletter: "The AI use case that saved me 10 hours this week"
- Community: "What's the most surprising way you've used AI in your work?"

### Change the Depth
- Full depth: blog post, YouTube, email
- Key insight only: X thread, LinkedIn, short-form video
- Single quotable moment: quote graphic, carousel slide
- Discussion starter: community post

### Change the CTA
- Blog: "Read the full guide"
- Email: "Reply with your experience"
- X: "Retweet if this resonated"
- LinkedIn: "What would you add to this list?"
- Community: "Drop your answer below"
- YouTube: "Subscribe for the next part"

## The Diminishing Returns Curve

Not every piece of content should become all 10 formats. Match effort to content quality:

| Content Quality | Recommended Formats | Why |
|----------------|--------------------|----|
| **Deep, original, data-backed** | All 10 | Strong enough to sustain every format |
| **Solid how-to or opinion** | 6-8 formats | Skip the formats where it's a weak fit |
| **Quick take or observation** | 3-4 formats | Thread + LinkedIn + community post. Don't stretch thin content. |
| **News reaction or timely comment** | 1-2 formats | Thread or short video. Speed matters more than breadth. |

**The test:** If you have to pad or repeat yourself to fill a format, the source content isn't strong enough for that format. Skip it.

## Platform Priority Matrix

When deciding where to focus, evaluate each platform on four dimensions:

| Dimension | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| **Existing audience** | Where do you already have followers/subscribers? |
| **Email conversion** | Which platform drives the most newsletter signups? |
| **Discovery** | Which platform surfaces your content to new people? |
| **Content lifespan** | How long does content stay relevant on this platform? |

### Typical Priority for Solo Creators

1. **Email** — Owned audience, highest ROI, longest lifespan
2. **LinkedIn** — Professional discovery, strong for B2B, good comment engagement
3. **X/Twitter** — Broad discovery, fast feedback loops, viral potential
4. **YouTube** — Highest effort but longest content lifespan (videos rank for years)
5. **Short-form video** — Best discovery for new audiences, but content dies in 48 hours
6. **Community** — Deepest relationships, but limited reach
7. **Blog/SEO** — Slow burn, but compounds over months
8. **Carousel** — LinkedIn-specific, high engagement when done well
9. **Podcast** — Niche but loyal audiences
10. **Quote graphics** — Supplementary, not primary

### Adjust Based On Your Business

- **Selling courses or consulting?** LinkedIn and email are your money platforms
- **Building a personal brand?** X and YouTube give you the broadest reach
- **Running a community?** Community posts and email are your core loop
- **Selling products?** Short-form video and SEO drive the most discovery

## Scheduling Principles

### Optimal Days and Times

| Platform | Best Days | Best Times | Worst Times |
|----------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| Email | Tue, Thu | 8-10am | Monday AM, Friday PM |
| LinkedIn | Tue-Thu | 8-10am | Weekends |
| X/Twitter | Mon-Fri | 8-10am, 6-8pm | Late night |
| YouTube | Mon-Fri | Morning upload | Weekends (for educational) |
| TikTok/Reels | Any day | Any time (algorithm-driven) | N/A |
| Community | Weekdays | Morning | Weekends |
| Blog/SEO | Any day | N/A (evergreen) | N/A |

### Weekly Rhythm

A sustainable content rhythm for a solo creator repurposing one source piece per week:

- **Monday:** Email newsletter (owned audience gets it first)
- **Tuesday:** LinkedIn post + X thread (social distribution)
- **Wednesday:** LinkedIn carousel + community post (engagement day)
- **Thursday:** YouTube upload (video day — filmed/recorded earlier)
- **Friday:** Short-form video clip + quote graphics (lighter production)
- **Weekend:** Rest. Schedule podcast points and blog summary for next week if needed.

### Volume Rules
- Never post more than 3 pieces per platform per day
- Leave at least one rest day per week (no content published anywhere)
- Quality > quantity on every platform. One great LinkedIn post beats five mediocre ones.

## Measuring What Works

After repurposing, track which formats drive actual results:

| Metric | What It Tells You |
|--------|--------------------|
| **Email signups per format** | Which platform sends people to your owned channel |
| **Engagement rate** | Which formats your audience actually interacts with |
| **Reach / impressions** | Which platforms show your content to new people |
| **Click-through rate** | Which formats drive people to take action |
| **Time spent** | Which formats people actually read/watch (vs. scroll past) |

**The only metric that truly matters:** Did this repurposed content grow your email list? Email is the only channel you fully own. Everything else is rented.
