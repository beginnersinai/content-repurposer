# Hook Patterns

Proven opening patterns for each content format. The format-writer agent should select the hook pattern that best fits the source content and target platform.

---

## The 8 Hook Patterns

### 1. Stat-Lead

Open with a surprising number. Numbers stop the scroll because they're concrete in a sea of vague claims.

**Template:** "[Surprising number]% of [audience] [do something wrong/miss something]. Here's what the other [remaining]% know."

**Examples:**
- "87% of small businesses use AI for exactly one thing. The ones using it for five things are making 3x more."
- "The average blog post gets repurposed zero times. The top 1% of creators turn each one into 10+ pieces."

**Best on:** X threads (hook tweet), LinkedIn (first line), short-form video (opening line)
**Weakest on:** Podcast (numbers need context when spoken), community posts (feels lecture-y)

---

### 2. Contrarian

Challenge conventional wisdom. People share content that makes them feel smarter than the mainstream.

**Template:** "Everyone says [common advice]. They're wrong. Here's why."

**Examples:**
- "Everyone says you need to post every day. That's the fastest path to content burnout, not growth."
- "The 'create 10x more content' advice is backwards. You need to distribute 10x more, not create 10x more."

**Best on:** X threads, LinkedIn, community posts (starts debates), short-form video
**Weakest on:** Email newsletter (subscribers already trust you, don't need to be contrarian to earn attention), blog summary (SEO-focused, not engagement-focused)

---

### 3. Story-Open

Start with a micro-narrative. Stories are the oldest attention technology humans have.

**Template:** "Last [time], I [did something]. What happened next changed how I think about [topic]."

**Examples:**
- "Last month I published one blog post. By Friday it was a Twitter thread, a LinkedIn carousel, two emails, and a YouTube video. Zero extra writing."
- "A client asked me why their blog gets 500 views but their business gets zero leads. I looked at their distribution and found the problem in 30 seconds."

**Best on:** Email newsletter (most personal format), podcast (stories are native to audio), YouTube (retention magnet), LinkedIn
**Weakest on:** Quote graphics (too long), blog summary (space-constrained)

---

### 4. Question-Hook

Ask something the reader cannot scroll past. Good questions create an open loop the brain wants to close.

**Template:** "What would change if you could [desirable outcome] in [surprisingly short time]?"

**Examples:**
- "What if every article you wrote automatically became content for 10 platforms?"
- "How many potential followers are you leaving on the table by only publishing on one platform?"

**Best on:** Community posts (invites response naturally), YouTube (curiosity = retention), email (earns the open), short-form video
**Weakest on:** Quote graphics (questions don't make great visual quotes), blog summary (needs statements, not questions)

---

### 5. Result-First

Lead with the outcome. Skip the story, skip the context — just show what happened.

**Template:** "I [achieved result] by [doing thing]. Here's the exact process."

**Examples:**
- "I turned one 1,500-word blog post into 47 pieces of content across 6 platforms. Here's how."
- "We grew from 200 to 2,000 newsletter subscribers in 60 days. The secret wasn't writing more — it was distributing smarter."

**Best on:** X threads (specific results get massive engagement), LinkedIn (social proof), YouTube (promised value), short-form video
**Weakest on:** Podcast (needs more warmup), community posts (can feel braggy without proper framing)

---

### 6. Gap-Reveal

Show what people are missing. This pattern works because it validates what they're already doing while revealing the next step.

**Template:** "You're already doing [thing A]. But you're probably skipping [thing B], which is where the real [results] come from."

**Examples:**
- "You're already writing great content. But you're posting it once and moving on — that's leaving 90% of its value on the table."
- "You already know your audience is on LinkedIn. What you might not know is that carousels get 3x the engagement of text posts."

**Best on:** LinkedIn (validates professional audience), email newsletter (builds on existing relationship), carousel (great for slide 2), community posts
**Weakest on:** Short-form video (too nuanced for 60 seconds), quote graphics

---

### 7. List-Tease

Promise a specific number of things. Specificity signals value and creates commitment to finish.

**Template:** "[Number] things I [stopped doing / started doing / learned] that [result]."

**Examples:**
- "5 content formats that take less than 10 minutes to create from any blog post."
- "3 distribution mistakes that are keeping your best content invisible."

**Best on:** X threads (natural thread structure), LinkedIn carousel (natural slide structure), YouTube (creates section structure), blog summary
**Weakest on:** Email newsletter (lists feel less personal), podcast (lists are boring to listen to)

---

### 8. How-To Direct

Skip the setup entirely. Start with the instruction. Respects the reader's time and signals practical value.

**Template:** "Here's how to [do thing] in [timeframe]. Step 1: ..."

**Examples:**
- "Here's how to turn any blog post into a Twitter thread in 5 minutes. Step 1: Pull out the single most surprising sentence."
- "How to write a LinkedIn carousel in 10 minutes: Take your best article, break it into 8 slides, one idea each."

**Best on:** Short-form video (immediate value), X threads (practical content performs well), YouTube (high-intent viewers want how-tos), email newsletter
**Weakest on:** Community posts (too prescriptive for discussion), quote graphics (not quotable)

---

## Hook Selection Guide

| Content Type | Best Hook Patterns |
|-------------|-------------------|
| How-To / Tutorial | How-To Direct, List-Tease, Result-First |
| Opinion / Take | Contrarian, Question-Hook, Gap-Reveal |
| Research-Backed | Stat-Lead, Gap-Reveal, Contrarian |
| Story / Narrative | Story-Open, Result-First, Question-Hook |
| Listicle | List-Tease, How-To Direct, Stat-Lead |
| Case Study | Result-First, Story-Open, Stat-Lead |
| Comparison | Question-Hook, Gap-Reveal, Contrarian |
| Announcement | Result-First, Stat-Lead, How-To Direct |
